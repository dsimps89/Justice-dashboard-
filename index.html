⚠ IMPORTANT REMINDERS
  • Only a lawyer can represent you in the Superior Court of Justice.
  • You have the right to represent yourself.
  • Tell the court know ASAP if you want a lawyer.
  • If you don’t understand something, ask the judge.
  • All communication with the judge must go through the Trial Coordinator’s Office.
  • Always give a copy of documents to Crown counsel.

GLOSSARY (QUICK REFERENCE)

♙
Accused: A person charged with a criminal offence.
▣
Disclosure: Information the Crown must give you.
⚭
Voir Dire: A hearing without the jury to decide legal issues.
▤
Subpoena: A court order requiring a witness to attend and give evidence.
⚖
Onus / Burden of Proof: The responsibility to prove something.
▥
Beyond a Reasonable Doubt: The high standard the Crown must meet.
↩
Adjournment: A delay or postponement of a court proceeding.
▧
Exhibit: A document or object admitted as evidence.
♡
Hearsay: Statements made by someone not in court.

EMERGENCY / CAN’T ATTEND COURT?

Contact the courthouse IMMEDIATELY.

Failure to attend can result in a warrant, trial in absentia, and loss of rights.

COURT ETIQUETTE REMINDERS

• Stand when the judge enters or leaves.
• Address the judge as “Your Honour.”
• Do not interrupt. One person speaks at a time.

• No eating or drinking in court.
• Use “Mr./Ms.” for all. No first names.
*Times may vary. Listen for any changes.
